How to Lose Weight Fast — Safely and Sustainably

NF
NoxFit Editorial Team
June 17, 2026 · 13 min read · Reviewed by a Registered Dietitian

Everyone wants to know how to lose weight fast — but "fast" means different things depending on what approach you take. Crash diets can drop 3–5kg in a week, but most of that is water and muscle. Science-backed fast weight loss targets 0.5–1kg of actual fat per week — which, compounded over 8–12 weeks, produces visible, lasting results without wrecking your metabolism [1].

This guide covers the six strategies that consistently produce the fastest real fat loss, ranked by impact and supported by clinical research.

The Reality Check: How Fast Can You Actually Lose Fat?

One kilogram of body fat contains approximately 7,700 calories [1]. To lose 1kg of fat per week, you need a deficit of 1,100 calories per day — which, for most people, means eating almost nothing. That is why sustainable fast weight loss targets 0.5–1kg per week using a 550–750 kcal daily deficit.

Week 1 is the exception: a large initial drop of 1.5–3kg is common as glycogen stores deplete and water weight falls. After that, fat loss stabilises at 0.5–1kg per week on a well-structured plan.

💡 The fastest safe rate: 1% of bodyweight per week. For a 80kg person, that is 0.8kg/week. Faster than this consistently leads to muscle loss, metabolic slowdown, and rebound weight gain [2].

6 Strategies That Produce the Fastest Results

01
Create a 500–750 kcal Daily Deficit
The single most impactful lever. Not too aggressive (no muscle loss) but large enough to lose 0.5–0.75kg/week of pure fat.
02
Eat 1.6–2.2g Protein Per kg of Bodyweight
High protein preserves muscle during a deficit, boosts metabolism via thermic effect, and kills hunger — the three biggest obstacles to fast weight loss.
03
Cut Liquid Calories Completely
Alcohol, juice, lattes, and sugary drinks are the most common hidden calorie sources. Switching to water, black coffee, and tea can cut 200–400 kcal/day immediately.
04
Add Resistance Training 3x Per Week
Lifting weights preserves and builds muscle, which raises your resting metabolism. More muscle = more calories burned at rest, 24/7.
05
Increase NEAT (Non-Exercise Activity)
Steps, standing, fidgeting. NEAT burns 200–800 extra kcal/day depending on lifestyle. Walking 8,000+ steps burns an extra 300–400 kcal without "exercising".
06
Track Food for At Least 14 Days
Studies show food trackers lose twice as much weight as non-trackers. You cannot optimise what you do not measure. Use NoxFit to scan and log in seconds.

What to Do in Week 1 — Your Action Plan

  1. Calculate your TDEE and subtract 500–750 kcal to find your daily calorie target. (Use our free calculator →)
  2. Set a protein target of 1.6g × your bodyweight in kg. Build every meal around a protein source.
  3. Remove liquid calories — swap juice, alcohol, and sweetened drinks for water or black coffee for 7 days.
  4. Walk 8,000+ steps every day. No gym required. This alone adds a ~300 kcal daily burn.
  5. Track every meal using NoxFit for at least 7 days. People who track lose 2× more weight than those who don't [3].
  6. Weigh yourself every morning after waking. Take a weekly average — daily fluctuations of 0.5–2kg from water are normal and misleading.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much weight can I realistically lose in a month? +
On a 500–750 kcal daily deficit with high protein intake: 2–4 kg in the first month (including initial water weight drop), then 2–3 kg per month of actual fat thereafter. Anyone promising more than 4 kg of fat per month is selling you water weight and muscle loss.
Is it possible to lose weight in 7 days? +
Yes — but most of the loss in week 1 is water weight and glycogen, not fat. A low-carb, high-protein diet can drop 1.5–3kg on the scale in 7 days. About 0.5–0.75kg of that will be actual fat. The rest will return if you eat carbs again — which is fine, that is not failure, that is physiology.
Does eating less than 1,200 calories make you lose weight faster? +
Temporarily, yes — but at a significant cost. Below 1,200 kcal, the body increasingly turns to muscle for fuel, and adaptive thermogenesis reduces your metabolic rate. Studies show that crash dieters regain 50–80% of lost weight within 2 years. A moderate deficit is faster in the long run because you keep it off.
What is the single best exercise to lose weight fast? +
Walking — because it is sustainable every day and low-recovery cost, unlike HIIT or heavy lifting. 10,000 steps burns roughly 400–500 kcal for an 80kg person. Combined with resistance training 3× per week to preserve muscle, this is the most effective exercise combination for fast fat loss without burnout.
