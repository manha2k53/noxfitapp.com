How Much Protein Per Day Do You Actually Need?

NF
NoxFit Editorial Team
June 22, 2026 · 11 min read · Reviewed by a Registered Dietitian

The question of how much protein per day you need has a clear answer from the research — but it differs significantly by goal. The government's minimum RDA of 0.8g/kg/day is enough to prevent deficiency but falls well short of what's optimal for weight loss, muscle building, or healthy aging. This guide gives you the right number for your specific goal, a free calculator, and a practical breakdown of how to hit it across meals.

The government RDA of 0.8g/kg is designed to prevent muscle wasting in sedentary individuals — not to support body composition goals. A 2020 meta-analysis of 49 studies found that protein intakes above 1.62g/kg produced no additional muscle mass benefits for resistance training individuals, suggesting 1.6–2.2g/kg covers nearly all scenarios [1].

For weight loss specifically, higher protein (1.8–2.4g/kg) during a calorie deficit is beneficial because it reduces muscle loss, suppresses hunger more effectively than carbs or fat, and raises the thermic effect of food (burning 20–30% of protein calories in digestion) [2].

💡 Quick reference: For most people, a target of body weight in kg × 2 = grams of protein per day is a simple, practical rule that sits in the optimal range for both weight loss and muscle building. A 75 kg person should eat ~150g protein/day.

How to Spread Protein Across the Day

Research shows muscle protein synthesis is maximised by spreading protein across 3–5 meals of 25–40g each, rather than consuming it in 1–2 large doses. A single meal can only use approximately 25–40g of protein for muscle building — the rest goes to other uses [3].

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 100g of protein a day enough? +
It depends on your bodyweight. For a 60–65 kg person focused on weight loss, 100g/day (1.6g/kg) is adequate. For anyone heavier or training hard, 100g/day is likely insufficient. A 80 kg person doing resistance training should target 130–176g/day. Use the calculator above for your specific number.
Can you eat too much protein? +
In healthy individuals with normal kidney function, very high protein intakes (up to 3.5g/kg/day) have been shown to be safe in studies. The claim that high protein damages kidneys applies to people with pre-existing kidney disease — not healthy adults. The practical limit is how much protein you can fit in your calorie budget while hitting other macro needs.
How much protein do I need per meal? +
25–40g per meal is the sweet spot for maximising muscle protein synthesis based on current research. Below 20g is insufficient to trigger a maximal anabolic response; above 40g provides diminishing returns for muscle building (though the extra protein still contributes to your daily total and satiety). Aim for a protein source at every meal providing at least 25g.
Do I need protein powder to hit my protein target? +
No — protein powder is a convenient supplement, not a necessity. All your protein can come from whole foods (chicken, fish, eggs, dairy, legumes). Protein powder is useful when you struggle to hit targets through food alone or need a portable option. If you can hit 1.6–2.2g/kg from food, powder adds nothing that food doesn't already provide.
