30 Best High-Protein Foods:
Complete List with Macros

NoxFit Team · 8 min read · Jul 2026

Hitting your protein target is the single most important nutritional factor for weight loss and muscle preservation. But eating chicken breast every day gets old fast. This is a complete list of the best protein sources — animal and plant — with exact macros per 100g so you can build variety into your diet without losing track of your numbers.

Why protein is the priority macro: Protein has a 25–30% thermic effect (you burn calories just digesting it), preserves muscle during a calorie deficit, and is the most satiating macro per calorie. Most people trying to lose weight eat 60–90g protein per day — research suggests 140–180g is where the real benefits kick in.

  • Prioritize protein at every meal, not just dinner. Spreading protein across 3–4 meals maximizes muscle protein synthesis better than loading it into one meal. Aim for 30–50g per meal.
  • Combine plant proteins to get complete amino acids. Rice + beans, pita + hummus, oatmeal + hemp seeds — these classic combinations cover all essential amino acids that individual plant foods lack.
  • Cheapest high-protein options: eggs, canned tuna, lentils, canned sardines. You don't need expensive protein to hit targets. These four foods can cover most of your protein needs for under $2/day.
