20 Best Foods for Weight Loss, Ranked by Science

NF
NoxFit Editorial Team
June 16, 2026 · 13 min read · Reviewed by a Registered Dietitian

Not all calories are equal. Two foods with the same calorie count can have completely different effects on hunger, metabolism, and body composition. The best foods for weight loss share three traits: they are high in protein or fibre, they have a low calorie density, and they keep you full long enough to maintain a deficit without willpower battles.

This list is ranked by a composite score combining satiety index, protein content, calorie density, and evidence from human clinical trials. Every food is backed by at least one peer-reviewed study.

How We Ranked These Foods

Each food was scored across four dimensions [1]:

  • Satiety Index (SI): How full you feel per 240 kcal, relative to white bread (SI = 100). Developed by Holt et al. (1995) at the University of Sydney.
  • Protein density: Grams of protein per 100 kcal.
  • Calorie density: Calories per 100g. Lower = better for volume eating.
  • Evidence quality: Whether randomised controlled trials (RCTs) specifically link this food to reduced body weight.
💡 Key principle: A diet rich in high-satiety foods lets you eat enough to feel satisfied while staying in a calorie deficit — the only scientifically proven mechanism for fat loss [2].

Top Protein Sources for Weight Loss

Protein has the highest satiety per calorie of any macronutrient. It also requires more energy to digest — 20–30% of its calories are burned during digestion vs. 5–10% for carbs and 0–3% for fat — a process called the thermic effect of food [3].

🥚
Eggs
SI: 150 · 6g protein each
A 2008 RCT found eating eggs at breakfast reduced calorie intake by 418 kcal over 36 hours vs. a bagel breakfast of equal calories.
🐟
Salmon & Oily Fish
SI: 225 · 20g protein/100g
Highest satiety index of any food tested. Omega-3s also reduce inflammation and support metabolic rate.
🍗
Chicken Breast
SI: 190 · 31g protein/100g
Extremely high protein density at low calorie cost — 165 kcal per 100g with 31g protein makes it unmatched for body composition.
🫙
Greek Yogurt
SI: 162 · 10g protein/100g
High in casein protein (slow-digesting), which suppresses appetite for longer. Probiotics may influence gut hormones linked to satiety.
🐟
Canned Tuna
SI: 180 · 26g protein/100g
One of the cheapest high-protein options at under $1.50/serving. 116 kcal per 100g — ideal for budget-conscious weight loss.
🧀
Cottage Cheese
SI: 150 · 11g protein/100g
98 kcal per 100g. High casein content makes it ideal as a late-night snack to reduce overnight hunger without adding significant calories.

Best Vegetables & Fruits for Weight Loss

Vegetables win on calorie density — you can eat a very large volume for very few calories, which physically fills the stomach and triggers stretch receptors that signal fullness [4].

🥦
Broccoli
34 kcal/100g · 2.8g fibre
One of the most nutrient-dense foods per calorie. High in sulforaphane, which has shown anti-obesity effects in animal studies.
🍎
Apples
SI: 197 · 4.4g fibre
A 2009 study found participants who ate an apple before lunch consumed 187 fewer calories than those who did not.
🫐
Berries
57 kcal/100g · high antioxidants
Low sugar, high fibre, extremely low calorie density. Polyphenols in blueberries have been shown to reduce abdominal fat accumulation.
🥬
Leafy Greens
15–25 kcal/100g
Spinach, kale, and arugula add almost zero calories while contributing fibre, magnesium, and folate. Eat as much as you want.

Smart Carb Choices

Carbohydrates are not the enemy — the wrong carbs are. High-fibre, low-glycaemic carbs slow digestion, stabilise blood sugar, and prevent the energy crashes that lead to overeating [5].

🌾
Oats
SI: 209 · 4g fibre/100g dry
Beta-glucan fibre forms a gel in the gut, slowing digestion and reducing post-meal hunger significantly compared to other breakfast cereals.
🍠
Sweet Potato
SI: 182 · 86 kcal/100g
High potassium reduces water retention. Slower-digesting than regular potatoes due to higher fibre content.
🫘
Lentils & Legumes
SI: 133 · 9g protein/100g
A 2016 meta-analysis of 21 RCTs found people who ate one serving of legumes per day lost 0.34 kg more vs. control groups.
🍚
Brown Rice
Lower GI than white rice
The bran layer slows glucose absorption. Substituting white rice for brown rice is associated with lower BMI in population studies.

How to Build a Meal Around These Foods

The most effective weight loss meals combine a high-satiety protein + a low-calorie-density vegetable + a slow-digesting carb. This triad hits all three fullness mechanisms simultaneously: protein satiety, stomach volume, and blood sugar stability.

Example: Salmon (200g) + broccoli (200g) + sweet potato (150g) = 520 kcal, 44g protein, 8g fibre. You will be physically full for 4–5 hours on just over 500 calories.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are there any foods that directly burn fat? +
No food burns fat directly. Claims about "fat-burning foods" usually refer to mild thermogenic effects (chilli, green tea, caffeine) that slightly raise metabolic rate — but these effects are too small to meaningfully impact weight loss. The real advantage of the foods on this list is that they help you maintain a calorie deficit by keeping you full longer.
Can I lose weight just by eating these foods without counting calories? +
Possibly. Building meals around high-satiety foods naturally reduces calorie intake without explicit counting. Studies on high-protein, high-fibre ad libitum diets show spontaneous reductions of 300–500 kcal/day. However, tracking for at least 2 weeks gives you a far clearer picture of where your calories actually come from.
Is avocado good for weight loss despite being high in fat? +
Yes, in moderation. Avocado's high monounsaturated fat and fibre content increases meal satisfaction. A 2019 study found that adding half an avocado to lunch reduced hunger over the following 3 hours by 40%. One serving is half an avocado (about 160 kcal) — keep portion size in check.
How many of these foods should I eat per day? +
Aim for at least one food from each category (protein, vegetable, smart carb) at every main meal. Variety within the list also ensures a broad range of micronutrients, which supports metabolic function and reduces food fatigue — a common reason people abandon their diet.
